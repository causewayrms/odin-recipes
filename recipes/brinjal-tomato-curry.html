Brinjal Tomato Curry

Description

This is an an easy and tasty South Indian curry recipe made with eggplant, tomato and assorted spices.

The recipe hails from Andhra and is known for its tangy and spicy taste. It is generally made and served with roti or chapathi, but tastes great when served as a side dish to the choice of rice recipes.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin / jeera
  • 1 tsp mustard
  • 1 chilli
  • Few curry leaves
  • 1 inch ginger (finely chopped)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 cup brinjal / eggplant (chopped)
  • 0.5 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp kashmiri red chilli powder
  • 0.5 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tomato (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)
  • Water and Salt

Steps

  1. In a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil and splutter 1 tsp mustard, 1 tsp cumin, few curry leaves.
  2. Add 1 onion, 1 chilli and saute until onions turn golden brown. Now add 2 cup brinjal and saute for a minute.
  3. Add turmeric, chilli powder, garam masala and 1 tsp salt. Saute on low flame until spices turn aromatic.
  4. Add 2 tomato and saute until everything is well combined.
  5. Cover and cook for 5 minutes, or until tomato turn soft and mushy. Now add 1 cup water and mix well adjusting consistency as required. Cover and simmer for 10 minutes or until everything is well cooked.
  6. Enjoy the dish.
